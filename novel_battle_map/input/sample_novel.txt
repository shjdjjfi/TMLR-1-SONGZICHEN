Chapter 1 - The River Crossing
At dawn, the northern regiment and two artillery battalions advanced along the old road toward the stone bridge.
Scouts reported enemy defenders digging in on the far river bank and placing machine guns near the ford.
The vanguard pushed through wet fields, then later converged near the bridge while engineers prepared smoke cover.
By noon the brigade attacked across the crossing under bombardment, and the southern troops tried to surround the lead companies.
After several hours of fierce engagement, reserve cavalry reinforced the right flank and occupied a ridge above the river.
The defenders withdrew to a village strongpoint at dusk as both sides exchanged fire near the road junction.

第二章 山口阻击
黎明时分，先锋旅沿着山口道路推进，守军在高地阵地布置炮兵与壕沟。
敌军试图从森林边缘逼近渡口，随后我方一个团后撤到山脊，再接着集结反击。
中午前后，双方在桥附近交火，炮击持续数小时后，援军部队从村庄北侧道路突袭。
黄昏前，前线连队占领河岸阵地并阻击敌军后续营队，夜间仍有零星交火。

Chapter 3 - Fireside Letters
The officers spent the evening writing letters and remembering home.
They discussed weather, food, and old academy stories beside a quiet stove.
No orders were issued, no formations changed, and no one moved beyond the camp fence.
Most of the chapter reflects on fear, friendship, and uncertainty rather than tactical action.
